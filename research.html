Research themes

Deployable perception systems for unstructured outdoor environments.

My research connects computer vision, signal processing, SLAM, GNSS/IMU/camera fusion, embedded systems, and AI to build robust field-deployable sensing systems.

A

Georeferenced 3D SLAM and Mapping

Metric-scale mapping of outdoor environments using visual SLAM, stereo geometry, Pose3 optimization, GNSS priors, loop-closure diagnostics, and robust factor-graph formulation.

Pose3 / SE(3)GNSS priorsGTSAM3D reconstruction
B

AI for Agriculture and Mining Robotics

Translating field robotics research across orchards, mines, farms, roads, and industrial environments: terrain mapping, yield estimation, worker safety, tunnel perception, and infrastructure inspection.

Agriculture 4.0Mining roboticsAutonomous inspection
C

Embedded Vision and Edge AI

Hardware-aware perception pipelines for edge deployment on Jetson and related platforms, with emphasis on latency, power, real-time inference, sensor interfaces, and field robustness.

JetsonStereo camerasReal-time AISensors
D

Semantic Landmark-Based Mapping

Using meaningful field structures—apples, trunks, poles, rows, assets, and terrain features—as semantic constraints to reduce drift and improve map consistency.

Semantic SLAMLandmarksRobust optimization
