Open student projects

Join if you want to build serious perception systems.

I am looking for motivated B.Tech, M.Tech, and PhD students interested in computer vision, robotics, SLAM, sensor fusion, embedded AI, and real-world field deployment.

What I value

  • Strong fundamentals in signals, linear algebra, probability, programming, or electronics
  • Willingness to read papers and implement them carefully
  • Interest in real datasets, sensors, robots, and field deployment
  • Patience for debugging, experiments, documentation, and reproducibility

How to contact me

Email me with the subject line: Student Project Inquiry — [Your Area].

Include your CV, transcript if available, GitHub profile, and 3–5 lines explaining what you want to work on.

Suggested topics

Initial B.Tech, M.Tech, and PhD project directions.

Stereo visual SLAM with GNSS priors

Build and evaluate SLAM pipelines that combine camera trajectories with GNSS constraints.

3D reconstruction baselines

Compare VGGT, MASt3R, DROID-SLAM, COLMAP, and related methods for outdoor mapping.

Semantic landmark detection

Detect apples, trunks, poles, and row structures for stable mapping and counting.

Mine-road and tunnel mapping

Adapt field robotics perception stacks for mining and industrial environments.

Embedded AI on Jetson

Deploy detection, segmentation, depth, and SLAM components on edge hardware.

Factor-graph sensor fusion

Use Kalman filters, factor graphs, GNSS, IMU, camera, and possibly LiDAR/stereo data.
